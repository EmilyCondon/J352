Supreme Court of the United States Overtime

Emily R. Condon

The Supreme Court of the United States is the top court in the nation. Under Article III of the US Constitution, a judicary branch is formed. The Supreme Court, or SCOTUS, concsists of nine judges. With societal changes in the United States, the court has become increasingly more diverse since its birth.

The current court is lead by Chief Justice John Roberts, a 2005 appointee under President George W. Bush. The court has a conservative super-majority.
