Journalism Seniors Tyler Lochte and Jack Bowman create camaraderie through intramural sports

By Emily R. Condon

For journalism majors who cover sports, they usually find themselves on the sidelines or in press box. This spring semester, though, many found themselves on the basketball court and in the soccer field thanks to an intramural sports team: the Beta Tau Nu Ballers.

"I feel like we bonded super well as a team because we had an already established connection through journalism, so it was nice to be the athletes as opposed to covering athletes," Ben Strober, senior journalism major, said.

In November of the fall semester, super senior journalism major Jack Bowman had an idea: let's get the sports journalists onto the court. So him and his senior journalism major roommate, Tyler Lochte, launched an Instagram page to announce Lochte as the first draft to the team.

From there, more journalism majors joined the team. "Beta Tau Nu" originated from the fact that many of the journalists work for Big Ten Plus, the Big Ten Network's streaming service. They called their groupchat "BTN" for Big Ten Network, so decided to also name the team BTN, or Beta Tau Nu.

The list of journalists ont he roster grew. A handful of sophomores also joined the team, with the first draft of Daniel Stein. Some non-journalism majors, like business major Mo Lochte, brother of Tyler Lochte also joined the mix.

Read the Basketball Team Roster

"I think, sometimes, with journalism everyone can be pretty bogged down in jobs, work and clips, to the point where it can be easy to forget we’re still in college. Having that time to spend with friends is what that college experience is all about. Wouldn’t change a thing," Bowman said.

The basketball season kicked off in December with the team's first practice. Then once the spring semester stated, the team's first scrimmage was on Jan. 30th, and the team played its first regular season game on Feb. 4.

As the season progressed, the BTN Ballers populated their Instagram with game announcements, game recaps and other highlights from players. This grew their following, popularity and eventually, their crowds as other journalism majors came to the stands to cheer on their classmates.

"We all love to cover sports, so this gave us the rare opportunity to be in the thick of it rather than being on the outside looking in. To be able to do it with friends, and in the case of the Rovers, make more, was awesome. Everyone bought in and that made it so much more fun," Lochte said.

Lochte enjoyed the basketball team so much that when intramural soccer signups rolled around, he decided to get a team together. That's when he made the Beta Tau Nu Rovers, or "Mice".

Soccer, needing more players than basketball, required some outreach. Many of the sports journalists were preparing for additional responsibilities with the kickoff of baseball season, where many also work for the Maryland Baseball network. So Tyler added more players to the roster, many whom were friends of journalism majors.

The soccer season kicked off in early May, and in a similar fashion, the Rovers populated their newInstagram account. Lochte created a new logo and brand for the team and even commemorated the season with team awards.

Read the Soccer Team Roster

Jackson Watman was once of the underclassmen journalism majors that played on both teams. He shared that playing on the Ballers and Rovers added to his overall experience at the Philip Merrill College of Journalism by provding fun and mentorship.

"All the seniors have definitely made my experience at Merrill better. Having people who have been there done that helped me see what it’s like and what I could expect. Having the relationships that I had with then also slowed me opportunities within organization like WMUC [the sports radio station many of the players worked for]. They’ve been great mentors to us underclassman so that’s definitely added to my experience here.""

Bowman and Lochte's roommate, senior journalism major Jack Susanin got to see the Ballers and Rovers go from just an idea in their South Campus Commons apartment to a place where classmates could bond and come together. He spent the months as the coach for the ballers and a defender for the Rovers; his girlfriend, Rose Fernandes, even became the official team photographer.

Now as Susanin looks to graduate, he reflected on the team as a place to build relationships with journalism majors outside of their traditional spaces.

"I think it was cool to establish more lifelong friendships outside of journalism with my friends. While our seasons ended in disappointment, thanks in large part to some shaky officiating, I would change any of it," Susanin said. " Up the mice."
